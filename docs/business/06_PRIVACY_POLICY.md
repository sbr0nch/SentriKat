## 1. INTRODUCTION

SentriKat ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our vulnerability management software and related services.

---

## 2. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

### 2.1 Information You Provide

**Account Information:**
- Name, email address, company name
- Billing information (processed by third-party payment processor)
- Support correspondence

**License Information:**
- Installation ID (hardware identifier)
- License key and activation status
- Usage statistics (optional, anonymized)

### 2.2 Information Collected Automatically

**Portal/Website:**
- IP address, browser type, device information
- Pages visited, referral source
- Cookies and similar technologies

**Software (Self-Hosted):**
- The SentriKat software operates entirely on your infrastructure
- We do NOT collect or transmit your vulnerability data
- We do NOT collect your software inventory data
- We do NOT have access to your agents or endpoints

### 2.3 Information NOT Collected

We explicitly do NOT collect:
- Your CVE/vulnerability data
- Your software inventory
- Your agent/endpoint data
- Your user credentials
- Any data stored in your SentriKat installation

We use collected information to:

## 4. SELF-HOSTED ARCHITECTURE

### 4.1 Data Sovereignty

SentriKat is designed as a **self-hosted application**:
- All data remains on your infrastructure
- No data is transmitted to SentriKat servers
- You maintain complete control over your data
- Air-gapped installations are fully supported

### 4.2 External Connections (Optional)

The software may connect to external services for:
- **CISA KEV**: Fetch vulnerability data from cisa.gov
- **NVD API**: Query vulnerability database from nvd.nist.gov
- **EPSS**: Fetch exploit prediction scores from first.org

These connections:
- Do NOT transmit your data
- Only download public vulnerability information
- Can be disabled for air-gapped environments

### 4.3 License Validation

- License keys are validated locally using cryptographic verification
- No "phone home" to validate licenses
- Installation ID stays on your server
